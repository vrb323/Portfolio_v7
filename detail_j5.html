Use of Dashboards

In exploring the application of dashboards, I analyzed tuberculosis (TB) dashboards to determine their effectiveness in serving their intended purpose. Specifically, I compared the US TB dashboard and the global Stop TB Partnership dashboard, focusing on their design, functionality, and usability. The US TB dashboard presents data in a static pamphlet format, using numbers and visually appealing graphics. While it effectively communicates basic information, it lacks interactive features that could enhance data exploration.
In contrast, the Stop TB Partnership dashboard, which includes global data as well as country specific profiles like China, offers a more dynamic and interactive experience. Users can navigate detailed information, including TB incidence rates, treatment outcomes, and program performance, making it a powerful tool for decision-making and monitoring progress. However, finding a comprehensive, China-specific TB dashboard proved less promising, highlighting gaps in localized data representation.
Dashboards are invaluable in healthcare services, offering applications from management and production to supply chain optimization and financial planning. For example, interactive dashboards like the Stop TB Partnership's not only enhance data accessibility but also empower stakeholders to identify trends, allocate resources effectively, and drive informed actions. Expanding the use of such dashboards across sectors, including power, utilities, and finances, can significantly improve operational efficiency and decision-making processes.
